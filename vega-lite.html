Video Games Sales Data

Dataset Overview

The dataset videogames_wide.csv includes 16,598 video games released between 1980 and 2020. It records each game’s name, platform, genre, publisher, and sales across four regions (North America, Europe, Japan, and Others), along with total global sales measured in millions of units. This provides insight into how gaming trends evolved over time and which titles defined each era.

Average Global Sales
  • Mean: 0.54M copies per title
  • Median: 0.17M (skewed long tail)
  • Range: 0.01 – 82.74M copies
  • Standard Deviation: 1.56M

A high standard deviation means that sales figures vary widely from game to game. While most titles sell only a fraction of a million copies, some outliers perform far above average. This uneven distribution reveals the industry’s hit-driven nature.

Most Frequent Genres
  • 1. Action (3,316 titles)
  • 2. Sports (2,346 titles)
  • 3. Miscellaneous (1,739 titles)

These genres consistently top global sales and release counts, showing players’ preference for fast-paced, competitive, and accessible gameplay experiences.

Most Popular Platforms
  • 1. Nintendo DS (2,163 titles)
  • 2. PlayStation 2 (2,161 titles)
  • 3. PlayStation 3 (1,329 titles)

Consoles from the 2000s dominate both releases and total sales. The PS2, Wii, and DS marked a peak era for physical game sales before digital distribution reshaped the market.

Variables in the Dataset
  • Name: Categorical (the title of the video game).
  • Platform: Categorical (the console or system the game was released on e.g., PS2, Wii, DS).
  • Year: Discrete numerical (release year ranging from 1980 to 2020).
  • Genre: Categorical (game category such as Action, Sports, or Role-Playing).
  • Publisher: Categorical (the company that published the game).
  • NA_Sales, EU_Sales, JP_Sales, Other_Sales: Continuous numerical (regional sales measured in millions of units).
  • Global_Sales: Continuous numerical (total worldwide sales for all regions).

The mix of categorical and numerical variables allows for both classification-based and quantitative analysis. The Year and sales data provide measurable trends over time, while fields like Genre and Platform help compare how different segments of the gaming market perform globally.

1. Global Sales by Genre and Platform

Data Reflection

The heatmap shows that PlayStation 2, PlayStation 3, Wii, and DS dominate global sales, mainly in Action, Sports, and Shooter genres. These platforms thrived in the mid-2000s thanks to large game libraries and major franchises like FIFA and Call of Duty. In contrast, Nintendo’s consoles perform best in Platform and Sports genres, showing their strength in casual and family-oriented titles. Lower sales in Puzzle and Strategy genres indicate limited mainstream appeal for niche games.

2. Sales Over Time by Platform and Genre

Data Reflection

Sales trends reveal cyclical peaks tied to major console releases, with the PS2 and Wii reaching record highs through globally appealing Sports and Action titles. Handheld platforms like the DS and 3DS show smaller but steadier sales, reflecting a loyal audience for portable and Role-Playing games. In contrast, PC sales fluctuate modestly across genres, suggesting a more fragmented but consistent player base.

3. Regional Sales vs. Platform

Data Reflection

Regional analysis shows that North America dominates console sales, especially for the PS2, Xbox 360, and Wii, reflecting its strong preference for Action and Sports games. Japan contrasts sharply, with higher sales on handheld systems like the DS and PSP, demonstrating its cultural preference for portable and Role-Playing titles. Europe follows similar trends to North America, while emerging regions show gradual growth, signaling gaming’s global expansion.

4. Genre Preferences by Region

Data Reflection

This interactive chart reveals that Western markets (NA & EU) prioritize Action, Sports, and Shooter genres, driven by competitive and fast-paced gameplay. Japan, however, overwhelmingly favors Role-Playing games, underscoring its storytelling-driven gaming culture. Other regions mirror Western preferences but at smaller scales, suggesting rising global interest in mainstream genres and shared gaming trends.
